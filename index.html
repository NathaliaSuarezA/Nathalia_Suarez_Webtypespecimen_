Lora.

Lora is a freely accessible serif typeface distributed through Google Fonts. Its italic variants exhibit a distinctly calligraphic quality, which enhances their readability and aesthetic appeal on digital platforms. The typeface is available in four weights, each accompanied by corresponding italics.

Meet the Designers

Olga Karpushina

Olga Karpushina (b. Moscow, 1987) studied design at Moscow State Pedagogical University and at the British Higher School of Art & Design in Moscow. While a student, she designed Private Sans (2010) and later collaborated on Lora (2011) and Vidaloka (with Alexei Vanyashin). She also created a 3D humanist stencil face, Sirin Stencil, in 2012.

Alexei Vanyashin

Alexei Vanyashin (b. 1982) is a Russian type designer, typographic researcher, and educator, based in Almaty, Kazakhstan. He studied Type & Typography at the British Higher School of Art & Design and has worked extensively on Cyrillic extensions, collaborating with Google Fonts, Swiss Typefaces, and others. He founded the Cyreal Type Foundry and has held roles such as the Cyrillic Script Chair for the Granshan Foundation. Currently, he teaches typography at Almaty City Design School.
